Rachel

Rachel grew up in Southwestern Ontario (more Southwest than Taylor’s hometown!) and enjoyed a childhood of sports, summers with her family and friends at the cottage, and getting her family’s snowmobile stuck in many locations across Essex County. Initially moving to Ottawa to pursue her Political Science degree at Carleton University, she quickly realized that her true calling was designing things that are difficult to construct, and then leaving the engineers to do all the work.
And so began her career in Architecture.
After years of lack of sleep, excessive amounts of coffee, and many technical questions and math problems requiring the assistance of her in-house engineer, she has finished her attempt at getting all of the degrees and is happily working towards her professional licensure as an Architect.

And then....Mexis brought us together:

The fate that brought Rachel and I together was called Mexicali Rosas Byward-Market. A guy named Pepe hired us both around the same time. She as a bartender and me as a server. I imagined he did so because we both, so obviously, fit the profile of hot-spicy-latina living in whitey-land. From the moment we met, I could swear Rachel was made to be Latin; except she doesn't dance, but that is okay. To this day, I still think she is. In her, I see traits that constantly remind me of Ecuador, my culture and my own people; this is why she is so special to me. In my heart, she is family and I couldn't be prouder to accompany her and Taylor in this journey.
